Test Research G-TELP tests are becoming consistently better, more accessible,
and more convenient for you with our rigorous research

We are changing the landscape of testing by connecting traditional theory with techniques
supported by the latest research in language assessment, psychometrics, and more.
ITSC GROUP and G-TELP KOREA are deeply committed to improving English assessment(s) and other related issues. We provide full support to rigorous research studies and projects on English Linguistic and Education, academic publishing, international academic exchanges, and other methods of scholarly communication. We aim to give diverse and rich opportunities to all scholars and researchers to expand their horizons in mapping, evaluating and advancing high-quality English assessments and measurements both nationwide and worldwide.
Research topics of interest
  • G-TELP tests and academic progress/performance/achievements
  • G-TELP tests and employment and career development
  • G-TELP tests and communicative competence
  • Comparison with other English proficiency tests
  • G-TELP tests improvements and innovation
  • G-TELP tests and validity
  • G-TELP tests and reliability
  • G-TELP tests and practicality
  • G-TELP tests and fairness
  • G-TELP tests and technology
Detailed Goals
  • To disseminate accessible, practical, authentic information and knowledge on innovative English proficiency testing and assessment(s)
  • To help maximize academic capabilities of professional researchers to build new pathways in Second (Foreign) Language Acquisition and English education
  • To evaluate the reliability and validity of General Tests of English Language Proficiency (G-TELP)

Eligibility

Eligibility
University
  1. 1. Full-time faculty at a four-year university
  2. 2. Graduate/postgraduate student
  3. * Major requirements applicable to all candidates: English Learning and Testing, Second/Foreign Language Assessment, or other related field
School Elementary, middle, and high school teachers
Application period No set deadline (Applications will be accepted at any time during the year.)
Field Any topics related to G-TELP tests would be acceptable and any suggestions would be appreciated.

YYYY Dr. Yunjung Nam Georgia State Unibersitiy, Georgia, USA Stakeholders’ Beliefs About G-TELP Test as a Measure of English Proficiency Required for Professional Purposes.
ITSC Grant Recipient Reflect
ITSC Grant Recipient Reflect
I started working on this project with funding from ITSC. Because I have been interested in stakeholder beliefs and perceptions of language assessment, I proposed this project to ITSC as a small contribution to the test validation efforts of G-TELP One of the greatest lessons I learned from this project is that testing companies and language assessment researchers should make more active efforts to reach out to diverse stakeholder groups to collect more validity evidence from multiple sources and raise assessment literacy of the stakeholders. Once again, I appreciate the valuable opportunity ITSC has provided.
