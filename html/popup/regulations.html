Regulations on handling misconduct

In the G-TELP Korea Committee, the following actions are considered as misconduct and will be monitored on-site or online. In such cases, they will be handled in accordance with Article 8 of the G-TELP test regulations.
  1. 1. Actions subject to misconduct handling:
    1. (1) Using forged identification to take the test.
    2. (2) Falsifying and using score reports.
    3. (3) Engaging in or conspiring to take the test on behalf of others.
    4. (4) Memorizing, taking notes, recording, or leaking all or part of the test questions or answers through online or offline means, such as printing, distributing, selling, or lecturing.
    5. (5) Peeking at or showing others' test papers during the exam.
    6. (6) Taking the test while possessing electronic devices such as cell phones, smartwatches, or stopwatches.
    7. (7) Making markings that may imply misconduct on the test paper or using specific symbols.
    8. (8) Borrowing or requesting the use of correction tape.
    9. (9) Disobeying the invigilator's instructions or interfering with the test proceedings.
      1. 1. Failure to comply with proctor’s identity verification and personal belongings inspection requests.
      2. 2. Actions deemed as misconduct by the invigilator.
      3. 3. Carrying all non-permissible items or storing them in unauthorized places against proctor’s instructions will be considered as misconduct.
    10. (10) Transcribing or recording test questions and answers and disseminating or lecturing through the internet or printing.
    11. (11) Leaving the test venue without any valid reason during the test.
  2. 2. Actions Resulting in Invalidation of Scores:
    1. (1) Failure to present the required identification (prohibited from taking the test, subjected to removal from the test site).
    2. (2) Taking the test while possessing electronic devices such as cell phones, smartwatches, or stopwatches.
    3. (3) Activation of alarms or vibrations on electronic devices, such as cell phones, during the test.
    4. (4) Marking on the test materials after the test completion announcement.
    5. (5) Acquiring scores through any illegitimate means or attempts to take the test improperly.
    6. (6) Leaving the test site prematurely during the test.
  3. 3. Actions Resulting in the Suspension of Score Processing:
    1. (1) In case of a significant and abnormal increase in scores, the score processing will be suspended, and a verification process will be conducted to assess the similarity of answers with other test–takers and handwriting comparison to determine the presence of any misconduct.
    2. (2) If an investigation is ongoing, or if the test taker is under indictment, prosecution, or legal proceedings related to misconduct, the score processing will be suspended.
    3. (3) Test takers who are notified as suspected of misconduct and have their score processing suspended will have the option to retake the same test with the same test materials or take a different test with similar difficulty levels, on the date specified in the notification or within eight weeks from the date of notification to assess the presence of misconduct.
    4. (4) If there are objections to the judgement of the re-taken test results, an additional opportunity for one re-take will be granted. If the test-taker is still determined to have engaged in misconduct after the re-take, they will be finally identified as a misconduct participant and subjected to sanctions according to the misconduct handling regulations.
  4. 4. Handling of Misconduct participants: Misconduct participants will be treated as follows in accordance with Article 2, paragraph 5 of the G-TELP Korea Test administration and management regulations:
    1. 1. The test in question will be invalidated.
    2. 2. The individual will be formally notified by letter that they have been identified as a misconduct participant to the relevant institution.
    3. 3. The individual’s G-TELP test eligibility will be revoked for a period of two years from the date of examination.
    4. 4. In cases where misconduct is confirmed, the scores obtained, including any previously acquired scores, cannot be reissued.
    5. 5. Misconduct participants will be subject to prosecution and legal action under Copyright Act violations (Article 314) and obstruction of business (Article 314 of the Criminal Act).
* The test taker is considered to have agreed to these regulations from the moment they enter the test site, and all other matters related to the test, beyond what is specified in these regulations, as announced by the proctor and through broadcasts, also carry the same effect as these regulations.
